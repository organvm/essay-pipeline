# {{TITLE}}

## The thesis

State the position in one sentence, up front. No throat-clearing. The reader
should know exactly what you are claiming before paragraph two.

## Why it matters

The stakes. What follows if you are right — and what is currently being done as
if you were wrong.

## The argument

Build it in load-bearing steps. Each paragraph advances one premise toward the
conclusion.

1. {{Premise — established, not asserted.}}
2. {{Premise — building on the first.}}
3. {{Therefore — the inference.}}

## The strongest objection

State the best counter-argument in its most charitable form, then answer it. An
argument essay that ducks its strongest objection is not finished.

## What changes if you accept this

The practical consequence. Bring the abstraction back to something a reader can
act on.
